Definition

The Dixon algebra T = ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 is the 64-dimensional real algebra:

T = ℂ ⊗ ℍ ⊗ 𝕆, dim = 2·4·8 = 64 over ℝAut(T) ⊇ U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) (Standard Model gauge group)// Non-associative (inherits from 𝕆), but alternative// Three generations of fermions ↔ three octonionic associators
Geometric Intuition

Dixon's insight: tensor the three normed division algebras. The symmetry group of the result contains exactly the gauge group of the Standard Model. The three forces are not independent — they're three faces of one algebra.

Each factor encodes one force: ℂ → U(1) electromagnetism, ℍ → SU(2) weak, 𝕆 → SU(3) strong (via G₂ ⊂ Aut(𝕆)).

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Force density: coupling constants g₁, g₂, g₃ measure how strongly each gauge group acts. They run with energy (renormalization group). In the Dixon picture, they may be computable from the geometry of T — the relative sizes of ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆 in the tensor product. Unification = the three densities becoming equal at high energy.

MASTERGraduate · Three Generations
▶

The octonionic unit imaginary elements e₁…e₇ have an associator: [eᵢ,eⱼ,eₖ] = (eᵢeⱼ)eₖ − eᵢ(eⱼeₖ). There are exactly three independent associator directions (the three Fano plane triangles not passing through the identity). Dixon conjectures: three generations of particles = three octonionic associator classes.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing Dixon algebra T with the Cayley algebra (𝕆 alone). T = ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 is the full tensor product.
MISTAKE 02
Assuming the Standard Model derivation is complete — it's a compelling observation, not a proof.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting that non-associativity is inherited: (ab)c ≠ a(bc) in T due to 𝕆 factor.
