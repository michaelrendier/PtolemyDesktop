Definition

The Bianchi identity (second Bianchi): the Riemann curvature tensor satisfies:

∇λRρσμν + ∇μRρσνλ + ∇νRρσλμ = 0// Contracted form: ∇ᵘRᵤᵥ = ½∇ᵥR⟹ ∇ᵘ(Rᵤᵥ − ½gᵤᵥR) = 0 (Einstein tensor is divergence-free)// This makes Gμν=8πGTμν consistent: ∇ᵘTμν=0 automatically
Geometric Intuition

The Bianchi identity is a purely geometric fact about Riemannian manifolds — a consequence of the symmetries of the Riemann tensor. But contracted, it becomes the statement that the Einstein tensor Gμν = Rμν − ½gμνR is divergence-free.

Combined with Einstein's equation Gμν=8πGTμν, this forces energy-momentum conservation ∇μTμν=0 — not as an assumption, but as a geometric necessity.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Conservation density: ∇μTμν=0 is the covariant statement of local energy-momentum conservation. The Bianchi identity shows that conservation laws are not arbitrary — they're forced by the geometry of spacetime. Curvature automatically conserves what it curves around.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · First Bianchi Identity
▶

The first Bianchi identity: Rρ[σμν] = Rρσμν + Rρμνσ + Rρνσμ = 0 (total antisymmetrization on last 3 indices). This follows from Jacobi identity for the covariant derivative: [[∇μ,∇ν],∇λ] + cyclic = 0. The second Bianchi (above) follows from differentiating this.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing the first Bianchi identity (R_{σ[μνλ]}=0) with the second (∇_{[λ}R_{|ρσ|μν]}=0). Both hold, but the second gives conservation.
MISTAKE 02
Assuming ∇μTμν=0 is a separate physical law. In GR it's derived — forced by the Bianchi identity and Einstein's equation.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting that ∇μTμν=0 is covariant conservation, not ordinary ∂μTμν=0. The difference matters in curved spacetime.
