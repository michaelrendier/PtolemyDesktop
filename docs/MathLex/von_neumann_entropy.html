Definition

The von Neumann entropy of a quantum state density matrix ρ:

S(ρ) = −Tr(ρ log ρ) = −Σi λi log λi// λᵢ = eigenvalues of ρ (probabilities)S(ρ) = 0: pure state (ρ² = ρ)S(ρ) = log d: maximally mixed (ρ = I/d)
Geometric Intuition

Von Neumann entropy is the quantum analog of Shannon entropy. The eigenvalues λᵢ of ρ are classical probabilities for the eigenstate decomposition. So S = −Σλᵢ log λᵢ = Shannon entropy of the eigenvalue spectrum.

Key quantum feature: S(ρ_AB) can be less than S(ρ_A) or S(ρ_B) for entangled states — classical entropy cannot decrease on subsystems.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Entanglement density: S(ρ_A) for the reduced state ρ_A = Tr_B(|ψ⟩⟨ψ|) measures the entanglement between A and B. S_A=0 = no entanglement. S_A = log min(dim A, dim B) = maximal entanglement (Bell states). Area law: for ground states of gapped local Hamiltonians, S ∝ boundary area, not volume — the density of quantum correlations saturates at surfaces.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Entanglement Entropy
▶

Take two entangled qubits: |ψ⟩ = (|00⟩+|11⟩)/√2. Trace out qubit B: ρ_A = ½|0⟩⟨0| + ½|1⟩⟨1|. Eigenvalues: ½, ½. Von Neumann entropy: S = −½log½−½log½ = 1 bit = 1 ebit. Maximum entanglement for a qubit pair. If |ψ⟩ = |00⟩ (product state): ρ_A = |0⟩⟨0|, S = 0. No entanglement.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Holevo Bound
▶

Shannon entropy bounds classical information in quantum states. The Holevo bound: I_accessible ≤ S(Σpᵢρᵢ) − ΣpᵢS(ρᵢ). You can't extract more than χ = S(ρ_ensemble) − ΣpᵢS(ρᵢ) classical bits from a quantum ensemble by any measurement. Von Neumann entropy appears on both sides of the fundamental limit.

MASTERGraduate · Black Hole Information Paradox
▶

Hawking radiation appears thermal: S = log(number of microstates) → area/4. But unitary evolution preserves S. As a black hole evaporates, S(radiation) must eventually decrease (by unitarity), but Hawking's calculation gives S always increasing. The resolution (Page curve, island formula) involves quantum extremal surfaces and replica wormholes — von Neumann entropy of radiation follows the Page curve, not Hawking's monotone increase.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing von Neumann entropy (quantum, ρ is a matrix) with Shannon entropy (classical, pᵢ are probabilities). They agree when ρ is diagonal.
MISTAKE 02
Expecting subadditivity in the classical direction: S(A,B) ≤ S(A)+S(B) always (subadditive), but S(A,B) can be < S(A) quantum mechanically (strong subadditivity is the deep result).
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting that log ρ requires matrix logarithm, not elementwise log. Use eigenvalue decomposition.
