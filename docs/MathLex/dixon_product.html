Definition

The Dixon product is multiplication in the Dixon algebra T = ℂ ⊗ ℍ ⊗ 𝕆. Elements are 2×2 matrices over 𝕆:

T ≅ M2(𝕆) (2×2 octonion matrices)// dim T = 2·2·8 = 32 over ℝ, but as algebra over ℂ: dim = 16U(1) × SU(2) × SU(3) ⊂ Aut(T) (Standard Model gauge groups!)
Geometric Intuition — What It Does

Dixon's observation: the automorphism group of T contains U(1)×SU(2)×SU(3) — exactly the gauge group of the Standard Model of particle physics. The Dixon product is the multiplication that makes this work.

The product is non-associative (inheriting from 𝕆) but alternative — a weaker property that's enough to define consistent physics.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Gauge attraction: the Standard Model forces (electromagnetic, weak, strong) are all encoded as symmetries of the Dixon product. The three forces correspond to the three factors ℂ, ℍ, 𝕆. Their relative strengths — the coupling constants — may be computable from the geometry of T.

Three Levels of Understanding
NOVICE High School · Standard Model from One Algebra
▶

The Standard Model of physics needs three forces: U(1) electromagnetism, SU(2) weak force, SU(3) strong force. Dixon showed these three symmetry groups all live inside the symmetry group of a single 32-dimensional algebra T = ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆. This suggests the three forces are facets of one geometric structure.

JOURNEYMAN Collegiate · Tensor Product Structure
▶

T = ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 means elements are formal sums of a⊗b⊗c with a∈ℂ, b∈ℍ, c∈𝕆. Multiplication: (a⊗b⊗c)(a'⊗b'⊗c') = (aa')⊗(bb')⊗(cc') using products in each factor. The gauge groups emerge as the unitary groups of each factor: U(ℂ)=U(1), U(ℍ)=SU(2), U(𝕆)∋SU(3).

MASTER Graduate / PhD · Octonion Non-Associativity and Generations
▶

The three generations of fermions (electron/muon/tau families) may correspond to the three units of associator non-commutativity in 𝕆. The associator [x,y,z] = (xy)z − x(yz) ≠ 0 in 𝕆, but the Moufang identities constrain it. Each generation = one independent associator direction. This is Dixon's deepest conjecture.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing T=ℂ⊗ℍ⊗𝕆 with a tensor product in the linear algebra sense — it's an algebra tensor product, which includes the multiplication law.
MISTAKE 02
Assuming the Standard Model embedding is proven. It's a promising observation, not a derived theorem.
MISTAKE 03
Thinking Dixon product is associative. It inherits octonion non-associativity.
