Definition

The Galois group Gal(L/K) of a field extension L/K is the group of field automorphisms of L fixing K:

Gal(L/K) = {σ: L→L | σ is a field isomorphism, σ(k)=k ∀k∈K}|Gal(L/K)| = [L:K] (for Galois extensions)// A Galois extension is normal + separableGalois correspondence: subgroups ↔ intermediate fields (order-reversing)
Geometric Intuition

Given a polynomial f(x)∈K[x], its roots in the splitting field L are permuted by any field automorphism. The Galois group is the group of permutations of the roots that are compatible with all algebraic relations between them.

Example: f(x) = x²−2 over ℚ. Roots: ±√2. The only non-trivial automorphism is √2 ↦ −√2. So Gal(ℚ(√2)/ℚ) = ℤ/2ℤ.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Density of solutions: the Galois group measures the irreducible symmetry of a polynomial's roots. Solvability by radicals ↔ solvable Galois group (derived series reaches identity). Density of the group = complexity of the root structure. Symmetric group S₅ (non-solvable) = the reason quintics have no radical formula.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Why Quintics Are Unsolvable
▶

Quadratics, cubics, quartics all have formulas using +, −, ×, ÷, and nth roots. Why not quintics? Because their Galois group is S₅ (all 5! permutations of 5 roots), which is not solvable. Solvable means you can break it down into abelian pieces. S₅ contains A₅ which is simple (no normal subgroups). No abelian breakdown = no radical formula.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Galois Correspondence
▶

For a Galois extension L/K with group G = Gal(L/K), there's a perfect bijection: {subgroups H ≤ G} ↔ {intermediate fields K ⊆ F ⊆ L} by H ↦ L^H (fixed field of H). Larger subgroup ↔ smaller field. |G:H| = [L^H:K]. This correspondence is order-reversing and the most elegant theorem in classical algebra.

MASTERGraduate · Absolute Galois Group
▶

The absolute Galois group G_ℚ = Gal(ℚ̄/ℚ) is the most studied object in arithmetic geometry. It acts on all algebraic numbers simultaneously. Every elliptic curve over ℚ gives a 2-adic representation G_ℚ → GL₂(ℤ₂). The Langlands program is the attempt to classify all representations of G_ℚ via automorphic forms.

Face: Kryptos + Archimedes

Kryptos encryption uses Galois group structure: the charset permutation in the HyperWebster addressing scheme is a Galois automorphism of the character field. The encryption key = the generator of the Galois group of the character extension.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Assuming all field extensions are Galois. An extension must be both normal and separable to be Galois.
MISTAKE 02
Confusing the Galois group (automorphisms fixing K) with the full automorphism group of L.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting the Galois correspondence reverses order: larger subgroup = smaller fixed field.
