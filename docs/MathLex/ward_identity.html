Definition

The Ward identity is the quantum field theory statement of Noether's theorem: a symmetry of the action implies a conserved current:

∂μ⟨Jμ(x) O₁···Oₙ⟩ = 0 (away from operator insertions)qμMμ = 0 (photon 4-momentum contracted with amplitude)// In QED: ensures longitudinal photons decouple from S-matrix// Guarantees optical theorem and unitarity of S-matrix
Geometric Intuition

Every continuous symmetry of a quantum field theory leads to a Ward identity — a constraint on correlation functions and scattering amplitudes. For QED, gauge invariance gives qμMμ=0: the amplitude vanishes when the photon polarization is replaced by its momentum. This eliminates unphysical polarizations.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Amplitude density: Ward identities constrain which amplitudes can be nonzero. They're selection rules for scattering. The photon propagator has gauge-dependent terms, but Ward identities guarantee they cancel in physical observables. The physical density of states is gauge-invariant because the unphysical states have zero amplitude.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Longitudinal Photons Vanish
▶

A photon has 4 polarization components but only 2 are physical (transverse). Ward identity forces the other 2 to decouple: if you replace any external photon polarization εμ with its 4-momentum qμ, the amplitude M vanishes. This is how gauge symmetry kills the unphysical polarizations — they have zero coupling to everything physical.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Slavnov-Taylor Identities
▶

The non-abelian generalization (for Yang-Mills): Slavnov-Taylor identities. They involve ghost fields (Faddeev-Popov ghosts) and are more complex than the abelian Ward identity. They ensure that despite the gauge-fixing procedure (which breaks manifest gauge invariance), the physical amplitudes remain gauge-invariant.

MASTERGraduate · BRST Symmetry
▶

The modern unification: BRST (Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin) symmetry. After gauge fixing, the action has a nilpotent fermionic symmetry δ_BRST with δ²=0. Physical states are in the BRST cohomology (annihilated by δ but not equal to δ(something)). Ward identities = BRST cohomology relations. This is the algebraic framework underlying all gauge theory.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing Ward identity (consequence of symmetry) with Ward-Takahashi identity (full QED relation including contact terms at operator insertions).
MISTAKE 02
Assuming Ward identity means the amplitude is zero. It means qμMμ=0, not M=0.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting that Ward identities hold in every gauge — they're gauge-invariant statements about gauge-dependent quantities.
