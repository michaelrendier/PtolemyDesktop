Rydberg Formula

General hydrogen spectral series — the Balmer series generalized
DEFINITION

The Rydberg formula gives the wavelengths of all hydrogen spectral lines, generalizing Balmer's formula to all series.

RYDBERG FORMULA
1/λ = R_∞ · (1/n₁² − 1/n₂²)
R_∞ = 1.0974×10⁷ m⁻¹ (Rydberg constant), n₂ > n₁
RADIAN / ANGULAR FREQUENCY FORM
ω = c·R_∞·2π · (1/n₁² − 1/n₂²)

PTOLEMY CONNECTION
SMIP — SPECTRAL LAYER STRUCTURE

The Rydberg formula's discrete series structure is the physical prototype for SMIP's 12 spectral layers. n₁ corresponds to the destination layer; n₂ to the source layer. The series limit (n₂→∞) corresponds to the sedenion boundary — the point at which all higher-order information collapses into the base layer. The Rydberg constant R_∞ is the equivalent of the SMIP coupling constant g at the U(1) stratum.
