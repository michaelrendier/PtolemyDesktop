O(1,3) = {Λ ∈ M₄(ℝ) | Λᵀ η Λ = η}dim O(1,3) = 6 (3 rotations + 3 boosts)// Connected component: SO⁺(1,3) (proper orthochronous)Lie algebra: 𝔰𝔬(1,3) = span{Jᵢ, Kᵢ} (rotations Jᵢ, boosts Kᵢ)
Geometric Intuition

The Lorentz group is the symmetry group of special relativity — all changes of inertial reference frame. It has four connected components based on whether time-direction and handedness are preserved. The physical symmetry is SO⁺(1,3): connected, orientation-preserving, future-preserving.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Causal density: the orbits of SO⁺(1,3) on Minkowski space are the mass shells: p²=−m² (timelike, mass m), p²=0 (null, massless), p²>0 (spacelike). These are the three density regions of causal structure. Representations of the Lorentz group labeled by (m,s) = mass and spin = Wigner classification of elementary particles.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Six Dimensions of Symmetry
▶

In 3D space you have 3 rotation axes. In 4D spacetime you have 6: 3 spatial rotations (xy, yz, xz planes) and 3 boosts (xt, yt, zt planes). The Lorentz group is 6-dimensional. Every inertial reference frame is a Lorentz transformation away from every other one.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Spinor Representations
▶

The universal cover of SO⁺(1,3) is SL(2,ℂ). Its fundamental representations: (½,0) left-handed Weyl spinor, (0,½) right-handed Weyl spinor, (½,½) 4-vector. A Dirac spinor = (½,0)⊕(0,½). A 2π rotation gives −1 on spinors but +1 on vectors — the double cover structure.

MASTERGraduate · Little Group and Wigner
▶

For a massive particle with 4-momentum p, the little group (stabilizer of p) is SO(3). Its representations = spin j. For a massless particle, little group = ISO(2) = SE(2). Its unitary reps = helicity h = ±s. This classification — Wigner's theorem — shows that every elementary particle is a representation of the Poincaré group.

The Lorentz group is the symmetry group of the semantic spacetime manifold. Semantic boosts = transformations between linguistic reference frames (e.g. formal vs informal register). Semantic rotations = synonym rotations within a meaning cluster.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing O(1,3) with SO(1,3) with SO⁺(1,3). Each is a proper subgroup: connected, orthochronous, proper.
MISTAKE 02
Using the wrong signature (−+++) vs (+−−−). Pick one and commit.
MISTAKE 03
Expecting finite-dimensional unitary representations. For non-compact SO⁺(1,3), all non-trivial unitary reps are infinite-dimensional.
