Perturbation Series

Approximate solutions by expanding around a known exact solution
DEFINITION

Perturbation theory approximates solutions to intractable problems by treating them as small deformations of exactly solvable ones.

GENERAL EXPANSION
H = H₀ + λ·H′
E_n = E_n^(0) + λ·E_n^(1) + λ²·E_n^(2) + ...
FIRST ORDER CORRECTION
E_n^(1) = ⟨n^(0)|H′|n^(0)⟩
CONVERGENCE
Perturbation series are often asymptotic, not convergent — adding more terms eventually makes the approximation worse. The series is useful only in its first few terms. This is the Dyson argument: QED perturbation series diverges for any coupling strength.
RADIUS OF CONVERGENCE (RADIAN)
|λ| < min_{m≠n} |E_m^(0) − E_n^(0)| / ‖H′‖
RG FLOW CONNECTION

The renormalization group resums perturbation series into running couplings, converting a divergent series into a convergent flow equation.

RUNNING COUPLING (SMIP FORM)
α_NN(r) = g² / (4π·ℏ_NN·ln(r))
PTOLEMY CONNECTION
SMIP — NEURAL YANG-MILLS

The SMIP Neural Lagrangian's coupling term L_coup generates a perturbation series in the coupling constant g. The renormalization group flow α_NN(r) resums this series. Standard backpropagation is the zeroth-order (Abelian, λ→0) limit of this expansion — gradient descent is perturbation theory truncated at first order with no resummation.
