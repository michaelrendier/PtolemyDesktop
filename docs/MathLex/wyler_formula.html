Wyler Formula

Geometric derivation of the fine structure constant α from symmetric spaces
DEFINITION

Armand Wyler (1969) proposed a formula deriving the fine structure constant α purely from the volumes of symmetric spaces associated with the SO(3,2) and SO(4,2) groups.

WYLER FORMULA
α = (9/(8π⁴)) · (π⁵/2⁴·5!)^{1/4} ≈ 1/137.036
NUMERICAL RESULT
α_Wyler ≈ 1/137.0360825...
Experimental: α⁻¹ = 137.035999084(21)
GEOMETRIC DERIVATION

Wyler's argument: the fine structure constant is the ratio of volumes of two homogeneous spaces associated with the conformal group of spacetime.

STATUS
The derivation is not accepted physics — the choice of symmetric spaces is not derived from first principles. However, the numerical accuracy (within experimental error) is not accidental and remains unexplained.
PTOLEMY CONNECTION
AINULINDALË CONJECTURE — A_π CONSTANT

The Wyler formula is relevant to the Ainulindalë Conjecture because the fine structure constant A_π ≈ 1/137.036 appears as the lower bound of the SMIP operator domain. The Wyler formula suggests this constant has a geometric origin in the volumes of the exact conformal groups that define the Cayley-Dickson tower's symmetry structure. If Wyler's geometric spaces are the correct ones, A_π is not a free parameter but a topological invariant of the algebra tower.
