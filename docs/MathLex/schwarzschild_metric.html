Definition

The Schwarzschild metric — exact solution for a non-rotating mass M:

ds² = −(1−rs/r)dt² + (1−rs/r)−1dr² + r²dΩ²rs = 2GM/c² (Schwarzschild radius)singularity at r = rs: coordinate singularity (event horizon)singularity at r = 0: true curvature singularity
Geometric Intuition — What It Does

The 1−rs/r factor does everything. When r>>rs: nearly flat Minkowski. As r→rs: the time component → 0 (time stops for a distant observer), the radial component → ∞ (radial distances stretch). At rs: the event horizon.

The event horizon is not a physical surface — a falling observer passes through it without noticing locally. But no signal can escape from inside.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Maximum gravitational density = the singularity at r=0 where curvature diverges. The Schwarzschild metric is the geometry of maximal pull. The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy S = A/4 = 4πrs² encodes the information density on the horizon surface — holographic: 3D volume encoded in 2D area.

Three Levels of Understanding
NOVICE High School · Frozen Stars and Black Holes
▶

From far away, watch something fall toward a black hole. You see it slow down, redden, and freeze at the horizon — time dilation makes it appear to stop. The infalling object sees nothing special at the horizon. Inside, space and time swap roles: r becomes time, and falling toward r=0 is as inevitable as time passing.

JOURNEYMAN Collegiate · Geodesics and Orbits
▶

In Schwarzschild geometry, the effective potential for radial motion is V_eff = (1−r_s/r)(1 + L²/r²). Circular orbits exist at r = 3r_s (photon sphere) and r = 3r_s (last stable orbit for massive particles — ISCO). Below ISCO, no stable orbits exist — matter spirals in. This is why accretion disks have inner edges.

MASTER Graduate / PhD · Kruskal Extension
▶

The Schwarzschild coordinates break at r=rs. The Kruskal-Szekeres coordinates (T,X) extend through the horizon: T²−X²=(1−r/r_s)e^(r/r_s). They reveal four regions: external universe, black hole interior, white hole interior, and a parallel universe. The singularity is a spacelike surface T²−X²=1.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Using Schwarzschild for rotating black holes — need Kerr metric instead. Schwarzschild is non-rotating only.
MISTAKE 02
Treating the coordinate singularity at r=r_s as physical. It's a coordinate artifact. Switch to Kruskal coordinates.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting that Schwarzschild vacuum solution has T_μν=0 — valid only outside the mass distribution.
