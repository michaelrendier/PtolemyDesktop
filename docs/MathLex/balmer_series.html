Balmer Series

Hydrogen emission lines — discrete energy transitions as geometric series
DEFINITION

The Balmer series describes the wavelengths of light emitted when hydrogen electrons transition from higher energy levels down to n=2.

BALMER FORMULA
1/λ = R_H · (1/2² − 1/n²)
where R_H = 1.097×10⁷ m⁻¹ (Rydberg constant), n = 3,4,5,...
RADIAN FORM — ANGULAR WAVENUMBER
k = 2πR_H · (1/4 − 1/n²)
GEOMETRIC STRUCTURE

The series is a discrete spectrum — only specific wavelengths appear. This is the first experimental evidence of quantized energy levels.

PTOLEMY CONNECTION
SMIP / HYPERWEBSTER

The Balmer series is the physical prototype for spectral layer addressing. Each hydrogen emission line corresponds to a discrete transition — exactly as HyperWebster's 12 spectral layers correspond to discrete information states. The series limit (364.6 nm) is the analog of the sedenion boundary: above it, all transitions collapse to the UV continuum (zero-divisor annihilation).
