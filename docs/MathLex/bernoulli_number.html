Bernoulli Numbers

Rational constants governing power sum formulas and the Riemann zeta function at even integers
DEFINITION

Bernoulli numbers B_n are a sequence of rational numbers with deep connections to number theory, topology, and the Riemann zeta function.

GENERATING FUNCTION
t/(e^t − 1) = ΣB_n · t^n / n!
FIRST VALUES
B₀=1, B₁=−1/2, B₂=1/6, B₄=−1/30, B₆=1/42
All odd B_n = 0 for n > 1
ZETA CONNECTION
RIEMANN ZETA AT EVEN INTEGERS
ζ(2n) = (−1)^(n+1) · (2π)^(2n) · B_(2n) / (2 · (2n)!)

Examples: ζ(2) = π²/6, ζ(4) = π⁴/90, ζ(6) = π⁶/945
RADIAN FORM — POWER SUMS
SUM OF k-TH POWERS
Σ_{j=1}^{n} j^k = (1/(k+1)) · Σ_{j=0}^{k} C(k+1,j) · B_j · n^(k+1−j)
PTOLEMY CONNECTION
SMIP / LORENZ-STIRLING

Bernoulli numbers appear in the Stirling polynomial used in the Lorenz-Stirling basin attractor system (§14 of smnnip_derivation_pure.py). The degree-9 Stirling polynomial's coefficients are expressed in terms of Bernoulli numbers. The eight Newton basins of the Output Engine root-find on a polynomial whose structure is determined by B₂ through B₈.
