Spectral Representation

Decomposing operators into eigenvalues and eigenvectors — the diagonalization of physics
DEFINITION

The spectral theorem states that every self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space can be written as an integral over its spectrum.

SPECTRAL DECOMPOSITION (DISCRETE)
A = Σ_n λ_n |n⟩⟨n|
λ_n = eigenvalues, |n⟩ = eigenvectors (orthonormal basis)
CONTINUOUS SPECTRUM (RADIAN)
A = ∫ λ dE(λ)
where E(λ) is the spectral measure (projection-valued)
PHYSICAL MEANING

In quantum mechanics, the spectrum of the Hamiltonian is the set of allowed energy levels. The eigenvectors are stationary states. Measurement = projection onto an eigenspace.

PTOLEMY CONNECTION
SMIP — LSH_DATATYPE / SELF-ADJOINT CLAIM

The LSH_Datatype's self-adjoint operator claim is a spectral representation claim: the HyperWebster address operator has real eigenvalues (real addresses) and an orthonormal eigenbasis (distinct words map to distinct addresses). This is the formal requirement that makes the addressing system invertible — the spectral theorem guarantees that the address → word mapping is unique iff the operator is self-adjoint. The open proof task in LSH_Datatype formalizes exactly this spectral decomposition.
