Definition

Shannon entropy of a probability distribution P = (p₁,…,pₙ):

H(P) = −Σi pi log pi (bits if log₂, nats if ln)0 ≤ H ≤ log n (max at uniform distribution)// Convention: 0·log 0 := 0H(X,Y) = H(X) + H(Y|X) (chain rule)
Geometric Intuition

Shannon entropy measures the average surprise of a distribution. High entropy = high uncertainty = more information content per sample. H=0 when one outcome is certain. H=log n when all n outcomes are equally likely — maximum uncertainty.

Shannon proved entropy is the unique function satisfying: continuity, symmetry, and that H(p₁,…,pₙ,0) = H(p₁,…,pₙ).

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Information density: H(P) bits is the minimum average code length needed to communicate outcomes of P. By Shannon's source coding theorem, you need at least H bits per symbol to compress losslessly. Entropy = irreducible information density. A fair coin has H=1 bit — 1 bit of irreducible uncertainty per flip.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Average Surprise
▶

If an event has probability p, its surprise is −log p (low probability = high surprise). Shannon entropy is the average surprise: Σ pᵢ·(−log pᵢ). Flipping a fair coin: H = −½log½−½log½ = 1 bit. If the coin always lands heads: H = 0. Maximum uncertainty = maximum entropy.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Mutual Information
▶

I(X;Y) = H(X) + H(Y) − H(X,Y) = H(X) − H(X|Y). Mutual information measures how much X and Y share. I(X;Y) = 0 iff X,Y independent. I(X;X) = H(X) (a variable shares everything with itself). It's symmetric: I(X;Y) = I(Y;X). It's the natural measure of correlation that doesn't assume linearity.

MASTERGraduate · Maximum Entropy Principle
▶

Given constraints (mean, variance, etc.), the distribution with maximum entropy is the least-biased choice — it assumes nothing beyond the constraints. Constraint: mean μ, variance σ² → Gaussian (max entropy over ℝ). Constraint: support on {0,…,n}, mean m → categorical. The Boltzmann distribution e^(−E/kT) maximizes entropy subject to fixed mean energy. MaxEnt = Bayesian prior from information theory.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Using log₂ vs ln without noting which. Bits (log₂) for information theory. Nats (ln) for physics and ML. They differ by a factor of ln(2)≈0.693.
MISTAKE 02
Forgetting the 0·log0=0 convention. As p→0, p log p → 0 — the contribution of impossible events is zero.
MISTAKE 03
Confusing entropy H(X) with cross-entropy H(P,Q) = −ΣP log Q. Cross-entropy ≥ H(P) by Jensen's inequality.
