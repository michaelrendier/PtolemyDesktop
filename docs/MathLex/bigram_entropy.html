INFORMATION THEORY

Bigram Entropy

Second-order statistical entropy — joint probability of character pairs
DEFINITION

Bigram entropy measures the average information content of character pairs in a sequence. It captures statistical dependencies that unigram entropy misses.

BIGRAM ENTROPY
H₂ = −Σp(x,y) · log₂ p(x,y)
CONDITIONAL FORM (RADIAN — NATS)
H(Y|X) = −Σp(x) · Σ p(y|x) · ln p(y|x)
RELATION TO UNIGRAM
CHAIN RULE OF ENTROPY
H(X,Y) = H(X) + H(Y|X)
Bigram entropy ≥ unigram entropy only if dependencies exist
For truly random sequences, H₂ = 2·H₁. Natural language: H₂ ≪ 2·H₁ due to massive bigram correlations.
DERIVATION

From the joint probability table p(x,y) over all character pairs in the charset:

p(x,y) = count(xy) / N_total_bigrams
H₂ = −Σ_{x,y} p(x,y) log₂ p(x,y)
PTOLEMY CONNECTION
HYPERWEBSTER / CHARSETPY

Bigram entropy is the metric used in charset.py to validate that the Unicode codepoint ordering produces a well-distributed address space. A low bigram entropy in the address sequence signals address clustering — a compression failure. Target: H₂ close to theoretical maximum for the charset size. CharacterNeuron uses bigram/trigram/quadgram convolutions — bigram entropy is the ground truth measure for those kernel widths.
