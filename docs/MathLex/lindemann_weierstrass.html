Definition

The Lindemann-Weierstrass theorem: if α₁,…,αₙ are distinct algebraic numbers, then e^α₁,…,e^αₙ are linearly independent over the algebraic numbers:

α₁,…,αₙ ∈ ℚ̄ distinct algebraic⟹ e^α₁,…,e^αₙ linearly independent over ℚ̄// Corollaries: π is transcendental (e^(iπ)+1=0 forces it)// e is transcendental (take α=1)
Geometric Intuition

The theorem says: once you exponentiate algebraic numbers, you get something transcendental — irreducibly non-algebraic. e^π, e^√2, and π^√2 are all transcendental. The exponential function "escapes" the algebraic world completely.

This proves most of the classical impossibility theorems: squaring the circle, transcendence of π.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Transcendental density: algebraic numbers are countable; transcendentals are uncountable — they have measure 1 on [0,1]. The algebraic numbers are a set of measure zero inside the reals. Lindemann-Weierstrass shows the exponential map carries the countable (algebraic) into the uncountable (transcendental) in the strongest possible sense.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Why π is Transcendental
▶

Euler's formula: e^(iπ) = −1, so e^(iπ)+1 = 0. If π were algebraic, then iπ would be algebraic. Lindemann-Weierstrass: e^(iπ) and e^0=1 would be linearly independent over the algebraics. But e^(iπ) = −1, which is algebraic and −1+1=0 — a linear relation. Contradiction. Therefore π is transcendental.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · What It Doesn't Prove
▶

Lindemann-Weierstrass proves many numbers transcendental, but leaves open famous questions: Is π+e algebraic? Is π/e algebraic? Is Euler's constant γ = lim(Hₙ − ln n) even irrational? These remain unsolved. The theorem covers e^α for algebraic α, but combinations like π+e are beyond current methods.

MASTERGraduate · Schanuel's Conjecture
▶

The grand generalization: if α₁,…,αₙ are ℚ-linearly independent, then the transcendence degree of ℚ(α₁,…,αₙ,e^α₁,…,e^αₙ) over ℚ is at least n. Lindemann-Weierstrass is the special case where αᵢ are algebraic. Schanuel's conjecture implies most algebraic independence results one could want — but it remains unproven.

Face: Archimedes + Kryptos

The transcendence of π appears in Kryptos's key generation. The irrational digits of π and e are used as pseudo-random streams. Lindemann-Weierstrass guarantees these streams are algebraically independent — they cannot be related by any polynomial identity.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing 'transcendental' (not a root of any polynomial over ℚ) with 'irrational' (not a ratio of integers). All transcendentals are irrational but not vice versa (√2 is irrational but algebraic).
MISTAKE 02
Assuming π+e is transcendental — it's believed to be but not proven.
MISTAKE 03
Thinking Lindemann-Weierstrass proves the impossibility of squaring the circle directly — it does, but you need to connect π-transcendence to the constructibility argument.
