Definition

The Yang-Mills equations for a gauge field Aaμ with gauge group G:

DμFμν = Jν (equation of motion)Fμν = ∂μAν−∂νAμ+g[Aμ,Aν] (field strength)DμFaνλ+DνFaλμ+DλFaμν = 0 (Bianchi)// [Aμ,Aν] term makes this non-linear — gluons self-interact
Geometric Intuition

Maxwell's equations are the abelian case G=U(1): Fμν=∂μAν−∂νAμ, no commutator term. Yang-Mills generalizes this to non-abelian G (SU(2), SU(3)). The commutator term makes the equations non-linear: gauge bosons (gluons, W/Z) carry charge and self-interact.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Force density: Fμν is the non-abelian field strength — a matrix-valued 2-form. Its energy density is Tr(FμνFμν). The commutator term [A,A] in F means the force field itself carries charge — unlike photons. This is why the strong force confines quarks: the field lines between quarks are themselves attracted to each other (gluon-gluon interaction).

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Self-Interacting Forces
▶

Electromagnetism: photons don't interact with each other (no self-coupling). Weak force and strong force: the gauge bosons interact. W bosons and Z bosons are charged and self-interact. Gluons carry color charge and interact strongly with each other. This is the [A,A] commutator term — it exists because the gauge group is non-abelian. Non-commutativity = self-interaction.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Instantons and Topology
▶

Yang-Mills theory in Euclidean 4-space has topological soliton solutions: instantons. They satisfy the self-dual equation Fμν = ★Fμν (field strength equals its Hodge dual). Instantons have action 8π²/g² and contribute non-perturbative effects. They're classified by the second Chern number ∫Tr(F∧F) ∈ ℤ — topological charge.

MASTERGraduate · Yang-Mills Existence: Millennium Problem
▶

Prove Yang-Mills theory exists on ℝ⁴ with a mass gap: a positive lower bound on the energy of any non-vacuum state. This is one of the seven Millennium Prize Problems. The classical theory is well-defined; the quantum theory (QCD) is believed to have a mass gap (explaining why there are no massless strong-force particles), but no mathematical proof exists.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing Yang-Mills (non-abelian) with Maxwell (abelian). The [A,A] term is absent in Maxwell — photons don't self-interact.
MISTAKE 02
Thinking Yang-Mills equations are linear. They're highly non-linear due to the commutator term and Dμ=∂μ+g[Aμ,·].
MISTAKE 03
Assuming the mass gap exists because QCD works experimentally. The mathematical proof is an open Millennium Problem.
