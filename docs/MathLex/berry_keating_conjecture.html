Definition

The Berry-Keating conjecture: there exists a self-adjoint operator Ĥ whose eigenvalues are the imaginary parts of the nontrivial Riemann zeros:

ζ(½+iEₙ) = 0 ⟺ Ĥψₙ = EₙψₙCandidate: Ĥ = xp+px = −iℏ(x∂ₓ + ½)// If such Ĥ exists and is self-adjoint → Riemann Hypothesis provenNo valid Ĥ has been proven to exist
Geometric Intuition

Self-adjoint operators have real eigenvalues. If the Riemann zeros are eigenvalues of a self-adjoint operator, they must lie on the critical line Re(s)=½. Finding Ĥ would prove the Riemann Hypothesis — one of the Millennium Prize Problems.

The strongest evidence: GUE statistics. The spacing distribution of Riemann zeros matches the eigenvalue spacing of large random Hermitian matrices — exactly the distribution for quantum chaotic systems.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Zero density: the number of zeros with 0 < Im(s) < T is N(T) ≈ (T/2π)log(T/2π) − T/2π. This is the density of quantum energy levels in a chaotic system growing logarithmically — the Weyl law for the conjectured Ĥ. The density of zeros encodes the density of primes via the explicit formula: Σ_{p^k ≤ x} log p = x − Σ_ρ x^ρ/ρ − log(2π).

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Primes and Quantum Energy Levels
▶

The zeros of the Riemann zeta function control the distribution of prime numbers. Berry and Keating said: what if prime numbers are the "quantum energy levels" of some physical system? The zeta zeros would then be the quantum eigenvalues. Proving this would prove the Riemann Hypothesis — worth $1 million and a place in history.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · GUE Statistics
▶

Andrew Odlyzko computed billions of Riemann zeros. Their spacing distribution matches perfectly the GUE (Gaussian Unitary Ensemble) from random matrix theory. GUE describes energy level statistics of quantum chaotic systems. The match is exact — but correlation ≠ proof. The missing piece is the actual Hamiltonian.

MASTERGraduate · de Bruijn-Newman Constant
▶

The de Bruijn-Newman constant Λ satisfies −∞ < Λ ≤ ½, and the Riemann Hypothesis ↔ Λ ≤ 0. In 2018, Rodgers and Tao proved Λ ≥ 0. Combined: Λ = 0 iff RH is true. The Riemann Hypothesis is now equivalent to a single real number equaling zero — a neat compression of the problem.

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing the Berry-Keating conjecture (the operator exists) with the Riemann Hypothesis (zeros on critical line). They're equivalent but framed differently.
MISTAKE 02
Assuming GUE statistics prove the conjecture. Statistical agreement is compelling evidence, not proof.
MISTAKE 03
Thinking Berry-Keating operator H=xp+px is the answer — it lacks the boundary conditions needed to give a discrete spectrum.
