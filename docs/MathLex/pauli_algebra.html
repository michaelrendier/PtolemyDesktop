Definition

The Pauli algebra is Cl(3,0) — the Clifford algebra of 3D Euclidean space, generated by the Pauli matrices:

σ₁ = [[0,1],[1,0]], σ₂ = [[0,-i],[i,0]], σ₃ = [[1,0],[0,-1]]{σᵢ,σⱼ} = 2δᵢⱼ·I (Clifford relation)σᵢσⱼ = δᵢⱼI + iεᵢⱼₖσₖ (combines dot and cross product)// Pauli algebra ≅ M₂(ℂ) — 2×2 complex matrices
Geometric Intuition

The three Pauli matrices are the three spatial basis vectors in the Clifford algebra of 3D space. Their products encode all of 3D geometry: dot products, cross products, rotations, reflections. They also represent spin-½ quantum observables — three orthogonal spin measurements.

Relational Analysis — Density · Pull · Attraction

Measurement density: |⟨ψ|σᵢ|ψ⟩|² is the probability of spin-up along axis i. The Bloch sphere is the space of spin-½ pure states — every point is a density-1 (pure) quantum state. The Pauli matrices are the generators of all spin rotations on this sphere.

Three Levels
NOVICEHigh School · Spin and the Bloch Sphere
▶

A spin-½ particle can be up, down, or any superposition. The Pauli matrices σ₁,σ₂,σ₃ measure spin along x, y, z axes. Their expected values trace out the Bloch sphere. A rotation of the physical system by angle θ around axis n̂ maps the state by e^(−iθ n̂·σ/2) — a 4π rotation returns to the original state.

JOURNEYMANCollegiate · Quaternion Isomorphism
▶

The even subalgebra of Cl(3,0) (generated by σᵢσⱼ) is isomorphic to ℍ: iσ₁σ₂≅k, iσ₂σ₃≅i, iσ₃σ₁≅j. The rotation group SU(2) ≅ unit quaternions ≅ even Cl(3,0). This is why quaternions rotate vectors: they're the Clifford algebra in disguise.

MASTERGraduate · Density Matrix Formalism
▶

A general quantum state (pure or mixed) is a density matrix ρ = ½(I + r⃗·σ⃗) where |r⃗|≤1. Pure states: |r|=1 (Bloch surface). Mixed states: |r|<1 (Bloch interior). Von Neumann entropy S = −Tr(ρ log ρ) = −½[(1+r)log½(1+r) + (1−r)log½(1−r)].

Common Mistakes
MISTAKE 01
Confusing Pauli matrices with Dirac gamma matrices. Pauli: 2×2, for spin-½ in 3D. Dirac: 4×4, for spin-½ in 4D spacetime.
MISTAKE 02
Applying Pauli algebra rotations with e^(iθσ/2) when you want physical rotation by θ — remember the factor of ½.
MISTAKE 03
Forgetting σᵢσⱼ = iεᵢⱼₖσₖ only for i≠j. For i=j, σᵢ² = I.
