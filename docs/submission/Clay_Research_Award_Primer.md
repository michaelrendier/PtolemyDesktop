# Clay Mathematics Institute — Research Award Primer

**Target:** Clay Research Award (annual)
**Status:** Horizon item — after Nature publication
**Note:** This is NOT the Millennium Prize. See distinction below.

---

## Critical Distinction: Two Separate Things at CMI

### 1. Millennium Prize Problems — NOT relevant here

The Millennium Prizes ($1,000,000 each) are for solving seven specific
named open problems that have resisted solution for decades:
P vs NP, Riemann Hypothesis, Yang-Mills Mass Gap, Navier-Stokes,
Hodge Conjecture, Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture, Poincare Conjecture
(last solved by Perelman in 2003, prize declined).

CMI does not accept direct submission of proposed solutions. The process
requires independent publication in a refereed journal, two years of
community scrutiny, and then CMI evaluation. None of these problems
are what the SMNNIP addresses.

### 2. Clay Research Award — RELEVANT here

The Clay Research Award is an annual prize recognizing major breakthroughs
in mathematical research. It is not limited to the Millennium Problems.
Past recipients include Alain Connes, Manjul Bhargava, and others working
at the intersection of mathematics and physics — exactly the territory
the SMNNIP occupies.

This is the correct CMI target after Nature publication.

## What the Clay Research Award Is

- Annual prize for major breakthroughs in mathematical research
- No application process — nominations and selections made by CMI's
  Scientific Advisory Committee
- Committee members (2024): Simon Donaldson, Michael Hopkins,
  Andrei Okounkov, Gigliola Staffilani, Andrew Wiles, Martin Bridson
- Not limited to any specific problem domain
- Has recognised work at the intersection of mathematics and physics
- Independent researcher status is not a disqualifier

The path is: publish in Nature (or equivalent) -> result achieves
community recognition -> CMI committee takes notice.
You do not submit to Clay. Clay comes to the work.

## Why SMNNIP Is Relevant to CMI After Publication

The algebra tower R -> C -> H -> O -> S with verified Noether conservation
and gauge group U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3) is pure mathematics with physical
implications. This is exactly the domain the Clay Research Award has
historically recognised — Alain Connes received it for work on
noncommutative geometry connecting mathematics and physics.
The SMNNIP result, once published and community-verified, sits in the
same territory.

The HyperWebster addressing system (Horner bijection into octonion space
via Cayley-Dickson construction) is itself a mathematical contribution
independent of the neural network application.

## The Sequence

1. Nature publication (or equivalent high-impact journal)
2. Community scrutiny period — other researchers verify and cite
3. Sufficient sigma weight and community recognition
4. CMI committee awareness — this happens through the literature, not application
5. Clay Research Award nomination arises from the committee

There is no form to fill out. The work creates the path.
