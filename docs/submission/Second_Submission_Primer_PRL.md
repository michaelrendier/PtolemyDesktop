# Second Submission Primer — Physical Review Letters

**Target:** *Physical Review Letters* (PRL)
**Status:** Immediate fallback if Nature desk-rejects
**Publisher:** American Physical Society (APS)
**Impact Factor:** ~9 (one of the highest in physics)

---

## What PRL Is

Physical Review Letters is the premier letter journal of the American
Physical Society. It publishes short, high-impact papers reporting
significant advances across all areas of physics. It is where the
Higgs boson discovery papers were published. It is where 5-sigma
results in physics belong when they have a physics-first framing.

If Nature desk-rejects (which happens to ~90% of all submissions,
irrespective of quality — it is an editorial scope decision, not a
scientific judgment), PRL is the immediate next submission. Same day.

---

## Why PRL Fits the SMNNIP Result

The SMNNIP result has a physics-primary framing available:

- Noether conservation verified in neural information propagation
- Gauge group U(1) x SU(2) x SU(3) identified as the structural constraint
- Algebra tower R -> C -> H -> O -> S (Dixon 1994) as the mathematical mechanism
- 7+ sigma confidence — above the physics discovery standard

These are physics claims. PRL publishes physics claims at this sigma level.
The neural network application is the domain; the physics is the result.

## Format Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Length | 4 printed pages maximum (~3,500 words + figures) |
| Abstract | 600 characters maximum (much tighter than Nature) |
| Structure | Introduction, results, discussion, methods (or supplemental) |
| Supplemental | Unlimited supplemental material allowed — full derivation goes here |
| Figures | Typically 3-4 for a 4-page letter |
| References | ~30 typical |
| Submission | journals.aps.org/prl |

---

## Key Differences from Nature

| | Nature Letter | PRL |
|---|---|---|
| Word count | ~1,500 | ~3,500 |
| Abstract | 150 words | 600 characters |
| Audience | All scientists | Physicists primarily |
| Review time | 1-4 months | 4-8 weeks typical |
| Desk reject rate | ~90% | ~50% |
| Framing | Cross-disciplinary | Physics-primary |
| Supplemental | Extended Data | Supplemental Material (unlimited) |

PRL has a faster review cycle and a lower desk-reject rate than Nature.
The supplemental material allowance means the full derivation engine
description and HyperWebster mathematics move to supplemental without
the 3,500-word main text constraint being violated.

---

## Reframing for PRL

The Nature submission leads with the cross-disciplinary claim.
For PRL the same result is framed physics-first:

Nature abstract lead:
"Neural network information propagation obeys conservation laws..."

PRL abstract lead:
"We demonstrate Noether conservation and gauge symmetry U(1)xSU(2)xSU(3)
in neural network information propagation, verified at 7+ sigma..."

Same result. Different entry point. Same figures, same Methods, same code.
The reframe is the abstract and introduction — approximately 2 hours of work
after a Nature desk rejection.

## PRL Submission Requirements

- ORCID: Required (same as Nature — one account covers both)
- Affiliation: Independent Researcher, Portland, OR, USA
- REVTeX format preferred (LaTeX template available at APS)
- Cover letter: 1 page explaining significance and fit for PRL
- Portal: journals.aps.org/prl/authors

---

## The Sequence After Nature Desk Rejection

1. Receive Nature decision (1-2 weeks)
2. Same day: rewrite abstract (~600 chars) and introduction (~500 words)
   to physics-primary framing
3. Submit to PRL via journals.aps.org/prl
4. Full derivation + HyperWebster math moves to Supplemental Material
5. Expect first decision in 4-8 weeks

---

## Why Not Submit to PRL First

Nature establishes broader priority and visibility. A result published
in Nature reaches all of science. PRL reaches physics primarily.
Given the cross-disciplinary nature of the SMNNIP result, Nature
maximises the impact of the first publication.

If Nature publishes it, PRL becomes irrelevant as a venue.
If Nature desk-rejects, PRL is the correct immediate next step —
not a consolation prize, but the correct physics venue for a
physics-primary result at this sigma level.
