# Alexander III of Macedon

**Born:** 20 or 21 July 356 BCE, Pella, Macedon  
**Died:** 10 or 11 June 323 BCE, Babylon (aged 32)  
**Title:** Alexander the Great — King of Macedon, Pharaoh of Egypt, King of Persia, Lord of Asia  
**Position in Ptolemy:** The origin. He gave us Ptolemy I Soter and placed Demetrius of Phaleron in Alexandria. Without Alexander, no Library. Without the Library, no Ptolemy.

## The Tomb

As of 2024–2026, Alexander's tomb is believed to be under the Siwa road district of Alexandria — slowly being excavated beneath existing skyscrapers. The site has been identified through ground-penetrating radar and subsurface archaeology. Full excavation is constrained by the modern city above it. He has been lost for two thousand years and is now being found one careful layer at a time, under other people's buildings.

This is appropriate. The greatest conqueror in history, buried under a city he founded, rediscovered by archaeologists with radar. The Library burned. The tomb hid. Both are coming back.

## Biography

Alexander was tutored by Aristotle from age 13 to 16 — receiving direct instruction from the greatest intellect of the ancient world. He absorbed it and then went to war. He took the throne of Macedon at 20 when his father Philip II was assassinated. He never lost a battle.

In thirteen years he conquered: Greece, Persia, Egypt, Bactria (modern Afghanistan), and reached the Indus River in modern Pakistan before his army refused to go further. He founded over twenty cities named Alexandria. The one in Egypt — founded 331 BCE — became the greatest city in the world.

He died in Babylon at 32. Cause unknown — fever, possibly typhoid, possibly poison, possibly grief after the death of his closest companion Hephaestion. His body was preserved in honey and eventually brought to Alexandria, where it was displayed in a golden sarcophagus. It was visited by Julius Caesar, Augustus, and Caligula. Then it disappeared from the historical record sometime in the 4th century CE.

## What He Gave Ptolemy

**Ptolemy I Soter** was one of Alexander's generals — his *somatophylax* (bodyguard), childhood friend, possibly half-brother. When Alexander died and his empire fragmented, Ptolemy took Egypt. He brought Alexander's body to Alexandria. He founded the dynasty. He funded the Library. He listened to Demetrius of Phaleron.

**Demetrius of Phaleron** came to Alexandria because Alexander's conquests had made it the intellectual capital of the world. The wealth that built the Library was Alexandrian conquest wealth. The scholars who filled it came because Alexandria was the center — and Alexandria was the center because Alexander made it so.

The chain: **Alexander → Ptolemy I → Demetrius → Library → Callimachus → the Pinakes → the first index of human knowledge.**

Every Face in Ptolemy the system traces to that chain.

## The Aristotle Connection

Alexander's education under Aristotle was not incidental. Aristotle taught him:
- Systematic observation over received authority
- The classification of all knowledge
- Medicine, rhetoric, philosophy, science

When Ptolemy I founded the Library, he modeled it on Aristotle's Lyceum — the institution Aristotle had built in Athens, which held the first known private library. Demetrius of Phaleron was a student of Theophrastus, who succeeded Aristotle. The Library of Alexandria was, institutionally, an Aristotelian project made possible by Alexandrian money.

Aristotle's library was reportedly acquired by Ptolemy's agents after Aristotle's school declined. It may have been among the first acquisitions of the Library of Alexandria. Alexander's tutor's books, in the library Alexander's general built, catalogued by the student of Aristotle's student.

## The Tomb — Current Status

The *Soma* — Alexander's mausoleum — is buried under a skyscraper apartment complex in central Alexandria. The building stands directly above it. Excavation is proceeding beneath and around the structure, constrained by the fact that people are living in the floors above the greatest archaeological find in the ancient world.

He conquered everything. He died at 32. He was buried in a golden sarcophagus in a city he founded. Two thousand years later he is under someone's apartment.
