## What the Formula Is Doing

A lacunary series is a power series with gaps — the exponents jump by a factor greater than 1:

```
f(z) = Σ_{k=0}^∞ a_{n_k} z^{n_k},   n_{k+1}/n_k ≥ λ > 1
```

The fractal is generated by iterating the partial sums of such a series and rendering the escape-time map. The key property: **lacunary series have the unit circle as a natural boundary** — they cannot be analytically continued beyond it.

---

## What the Math Is Actually Doing

The natural boundary property is a theorem due to Hadamard (1892). The proof: the gaps in the exponents cause the partial sums to oscillate wildly near every point of the unit circle — there is no analytic continuation because the function has essential singularities everywhere on the boundary, not just at isolated points.

This is profoundly different from a pole or a branch cut. A pole can be circumnavigated. A natural boundary cannot. The function simply does not exist beyond it, in any analytic sense.

The Riemann zeta function `ζ(s) = Σ n^{−s}` is not lacunary — all integers appear. But its **Mellin transform representation** and the associated L-functions do exhibit lacunary structure when restricted to specific arithmetic progressions. The connection is through the theory of **modular forms**, where gaps in the Fourier coefficients indicate deeper arithmetic structure.

The unit circle as natural boundary has a direct SMIP analog: the sedenion zero-divisor locus. Beyond the sedenion boundary, the division algebra property fails — the algebra "cannot be continued" in exactly the same sense that a lacunary series cannot be analytically continued past its natural boundary.
