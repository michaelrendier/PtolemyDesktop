## What the Formula Is Doing

Baker's transformation acts on the unit square by:

1. **Stretch** horizontally by factor 2, compress vertically by factor 1/2
2. **Fold** — if the point has gone outside [0,1], fold it back

The explicit map:

```
(x, y) → (2x mod 1,  (y + ⌊2x⌋) / 2)
```

This is the canonical example of a **hyperbolic dynamical system** — it has one expanding direction and one contracting direction at every point, with a constant expansion factor of 2.

---

## What the Math Is Actually Doing

The Baker map is **exactly** the two-sided shift on binary sequences. The x-coordinate encodes the future of the orbit (binary expansion of x), the y-coordinate encodes the past. The map shifts this sequence left by one position — forward in time.

This encoding is an **isomorphism**: the Baker map is, up to a change of coordinates, identical to the Bernoulli shift on {0,1}^ℤ. This means all the ergodic theory of the Bernoulli shift — mixing, entropy, decay of correlations — applies directly to the Baker map.

The **Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy** of the Baker map is exactly log 2 — one bit of information per iteration. This is the maximum entropy achievable for a map on the unit square with expansion factor 2.

The Baker map also has an explicit **symbolic dynamics**: every orbit corresponds to a unique bi-infinite binary sequence, and vice versa. This symbolic coding is the geometric foundation of the Markov partition — a partition of phase space into finitely many pieces such that the map's action on pieces can be described by a finite directed graph.

## Derivation Path

The J_N inversion `r → 1/r` in SMIP is the radial component of a two-stroke map: the **contraction** stroke (r → 1/r for r > 1 maps to r < 1) followed by the **expansion** stroke (subsequent Cayley-Dickson doubling). This two-stroke structure is exactly the Baker map.

**Derivation target 1 (Entropy of SMIP inversion):** The KS entropy of the Baker map is log 2 = one bit per iteration. The SMIP Inversion Engine applies J_N once per layer transition (8 transitions in the full tower ℝ→ℂ→ℍ→𝕆→𝕊). The total entropy generated by 8 Baker-map iterations is 8 bits — exactly the 8-component dimensionality of the octonion layer. This is not a coincidence: the octonion's 8 real components are the 8 bits generated by 8 applications of the two-stroke Baker-type map. **Derivation: show that the SMIP inversion sequence has KS entropy = log(dim_layer) at each Cayley-Dickson doubling step.**

**Derivation target 2 (Markov partition of SMIP):** If the SMIP inversion engine has a Markov partition, then its dynamics can be described by a finite directed graph — a **transition matrix**. The eigenvalues of this matrix give the mixing rates of information through the tower. The largest eigenvalue is the topological entropy. If this entropy equals the Shannon entropy of the HyperWebster address distribution, SMIP achieves maximum information throughput.

**Derivation target 3 (Symbolic dynamics = HyperWebster addresses):** The Baker map's symbolic coding assigns a binary sequence to every orbit. In SMIP, the HyperWebster address is a base-N sequence (N = charset size). If the SMIP inversion engine has the symbolic dynamics of a Baker-type map over alphabet size N, then HyperWebster addresses are exactly the symbolic codings of SMIP orbits. This would make the addressing system and the propagation geometry the same object viewed differently.
